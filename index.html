What is safeguarding?

Safeguarding for adults is about protecting an adult's right to live in safety, free from abuse and neglect. It involves organizations and professionals working together to prevent and stop the risks and experiences of abuse or neglect, considering the adult's wishes, feelings, and well-being.

Empowerment

People being supported and encouraged to make their own decisions and informed consent

Prevention

It is better to take action before harm occurs.

Proportionality

The least intrusive response appropriate to the risk presented.

Protection

Support and representation for those in greatest need

Partnership

Local solutions through services working with their communities. Communities have a part to play in preventing, detecting and reporting neglect and abuse.

Acountability

Accountability and transparency in safeguarding practice.

Recognizing the signs of safeguarding issues

Physical Abuse

  • Unexplained injuries, bruises, or burns.
  • Assault, hitting, slapping, punching, kicking, hair-pulling, biting, pushing.
  • Rough handling or inappropriate use of physical restraint.

Emotional Abuse

  • Excessive withdrawal, fear, or anxiety.
  • Sudden changes in behavior or personality.
  • Low self-esteem or self-worth.

Sexual Abuse

  • Inappropriate sexual behavior or knowledge for the person's age.
  • Unexplained soreness or bruises around the genital or anal areas.
  • Reluctance to be alone with a particular person.

Neglect

  • Poor hygiene.
  • Malnutrition.
  • Unattended medical needs.

Financial Abuse

  • Unexplained loss of money or possessions.
  • Sudden changes in financial situation.
  • Unpaid bills or lack of basic necessities despite having funds.

Discriminatory Abuse

  • Harassment or slurs based on race, gender, or disability.
  • Unequal treatment or denial of services based on protected characteristics.
  • Verbal abuse, derogatory remarks, or inappropriate jokes.

    • Institutional Abuse

    • Inadequate care in a residential or nursing home.
    • Lack of respect for personal dignity and privacy.
    • Overcrowding or poor living conditions.

    Self-neglect

    • Failure to attend to personal hygiene or health.
    • Living in unsanitary or unsafe conditions.
    • Hoarding items or animals.

    Action Steps:

    Steps to take when safeguarding issues arise.

    Step 1: Identify the Issue

    Recognize and acknowledge the signs of safeguarding issues.

    Step 2: Report the Concern

    Inform the relevant authorities or safeguarding officer about the issue.

    Step 3: Document the Incident

    Keep detailed records of the incident and any actions taken.

    Step 4: Follow Up

    Ensure that appropriate measures are taken and the issue is resolved.
